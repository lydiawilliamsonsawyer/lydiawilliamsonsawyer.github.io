## Current interests

My main interest is in the intersection between feelings and broader social and political conditions, see [projects page](https://lydiawsawyer.com/projects/). This is an interest which has led me in many directions, including:

**Spirituality and Buddhism**. I have spent time living in Buddhist communities and organised retreats with the Triratna Buddhist community on meditation and ecology. In 2024, I was involved in setting up a "Transforming Self and World" group at the London Buddhist Centre which involves regular public events to discuss the applicability of Buddhist teachings to current social and political issues.  

I am currently exploring what Rosi Braidotti has termed as the postsecular turn in feminism. I am interested in the contemporary resurgence and digital mediation of spiritual discourses amongst young women in a UK context, where conventional fantasies of the 'good life' are increasingly untenable. I am keen to explore why it is that White, middle-class spiritual practices are often framed as empowering and liberating, whilst racialised religious practices continue to be positioned as incompatible with modernity.

**Digital technologies**. I am interested in the processes through which discourses of optimisation, efficiency, and productivity are culturally mobilised to justify harmful and high-risk technological developments. I have recently written about the dangers of transitioning to a digital cashless economy for the LSE student newspaper, [forthcoming Jan 2026].

**Alternative, non-institutional learning**. I am passionate about accessible, participatory, and non-hierarchical forms of education. I engage with this interest through working at Kairos Counter-Club and as a member of the organising committee for AntiUniversity.

**Community living**. I am drawn to alternative forms of community living and have lived and volunteered at various communities, co-housing projects, and ecovillages in the UK and Europe. The role of Nonviolent Communication for building and sustaining communities is a particular interest.
